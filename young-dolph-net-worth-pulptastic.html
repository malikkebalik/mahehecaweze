What was Young Dolph’s net worth?

Young Dolph, the American rapper, producer, and recording artist, had a net worth of $3 million at the time of his death. He was born Adolph Robert Thornton Jr. on August 11, 1985, in Chicago, Illinois. Known for his gritty lyrics and tales of street life, Dolph gained popularity in the mid-2010s with successful mixtapes and albums that solidified his position in Southern hip-hop.

Dolph founded the record label Paper Route Empire and collaborated frequently with rapper Gucci Mane. Despite facing numerous legal challenges throughout his career, he remained dedicated to his craft and left a lasting impact on the rap community. Tragically, Dolph was shot and killed on November 17, 2021, in his hometown of Memphis, Tennessee, at the age of 36.

Early Life and Musical Influences

Young Dolph was raised in Memphis, Tennessee, where he faced poverty, crime, and limited opportunities. To cope with his difficult surroundings, he turned to music, specifically rap, as an outlet to express his experiences and emotions. Influenced by Southern rap legends such as Three 6 Mafia, 8Ball & MJG, and Playa Fly, as well as national acts like Jay-Z and Tupac Shakur, Dolph developed his unique style and voice in the industry.

Young Dolph’s Early Career

Young Dolph started his career by releasing mixtapes independently in 2008 with “Paper Route Campaign.” Over the years, he released various mixtapes, such as “Welcome 2 Dolph World” in 2010, “High Class Street Music” series from 2011 to 2014, and “South Memphis Kingpin” in 2013. However, it was “High Class Street Music 5: The Plug Best Friend” in 2015 that caught the attention of many and helped him establish his position in the Southern rap scene.

Young Dolph’s Discography

In 2016, Young Dolph’s debut album “King of Memphis” reached number 49 on the Billboard 200, featuring popular tracks like “Get Paid” and “It’s Goin Down.” The following year, he released “Bulletproof,” inspired by a shooting incident he survived in Charlotte. In 2017, Dolph collaborated with artists like Gucci Mane, Ty Dolla $ign, and DRAM on the album “Thinking Out Loud.”

Young Dolph’s Fierce Independence and Entrepreneurial Ventures

Throughout his career, Young Dolph remained fiercely independent, releasing his music through his own record label, Paper Route Empire (PRE). He built a loyal fan base and established himself as a successful entrepreneur by maintaining creative control.

Aside from his music career, Dolph also had various business ventures, including a partnership with a Memphis-based coffee company.

Philanthropic Efforts of Young Dolph

Young Dolph was known for his commitment to giving back to his community despite his tough exterior. He regularly donated to various causes, including providing scholarships to students and supporting families in need during the holidays.

Moreover, Dolph’s generosity extended to his fans as well. He gave away cars and other prizes to show his appreciation for his fans.

Young Dolph Survives 2017 Shooting Incident

In February 2017, Young Dolph was the target of gunfire when several dozen, potentially up to 100, rounds, were fired at him in Charlotte, North Carolina. He spent two weeks in the hospital recovering from his wounds.

Some speculated that the attack was connected to a rivalry with rapper Yo Gotti. Yo Gotti was named as a person of interest but was soon cleared.

Young Dolph chose the name Bulletproof for his second album based on the incident.

Young Dolph Tragically Murdered in 2021

On November 17, 2021, Young Dolph was tragically murdered in a shooting incident in Memphis, Tennessee.
