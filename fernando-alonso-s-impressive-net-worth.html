Fernando Alonso Diaz was born on 29 July, 1981, in Oviedo, Asturias, Spain. His father, an enthusiastic amateur kart racer, envisaged his son embracing his passion. Initially, he built a kart for Fernando’s older sister, Lorena. However, it was young Fernando who demonstrated a profound interest in the sport. With his father’s support, Fernando participated in various karting competitions in Spain. At the onset of his career, Alonso clinched four Spanish championships in the junior category, eventually claiming victory in the Junior World Cup in 1996. Despite the limited financial gains from this win, it served as a major encouragement to pursue his career. Two years later, he secured the second position in the European Championship.

Alonso embarked on his Formula One journey in 2001. He was one of the youngest drivers at the time when he made his debut with Minardi during the F-1 motor race at the Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit (Australian Grand Prix 2001) on 4 March 2001. Over a decade of successful driving and competition, Alonso has not only augmented his net worth but also clinched top positions in the F1 Championship while representing the Scuderia Ferrari team. Securing victories in prestigious events like Formula One significantly propelled Fernando Alonso’s net worth.

In November 2006, Alonso tied the knot with Raquel del Rosario Macias, a popular Spanish singer and the leader of the band El Sueño de Morfeo. However, their marriage was short-lived, culminating in a divorce after five years of togetherness, in December 2011.

Fernando Alonso assumed the role of Goodwill Ambassador for UNICEF (The United Nations Children’s Fund), extending long-term humanitarian assistance to children and their mothers in developing nations.
