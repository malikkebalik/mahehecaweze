Despite continuing to be an independent artist, he has been able to capture the attention of the younger generation and win acclaim all around the world. Skepta draws sizable audiences to festivals and gatherings all over the world.

Where was Skepta born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Joseph Junior Adenuga, well known by his stage name Skepta, was born in Tottenham, North London, United Kingdom. His parents, Ivy and Joseph Adenuga are immigrants from Nigeria who moved to England to give their children a better life.

His mother wrote a memoir about leaving the civil war in Nigeria. Skepta’s nationality is American, and he is of Mixed ethnicity.

He was the oldest of four children and was raised on the Meridian Walk Estate in Tottenham. Jme, who goes by the stage name Jamie Adenuga, is a musician. Skepta pursued his education at Winchmore Council School.

How did Skepta start his Professional Career?

Skepta got his start in music as a DJ on illegal radio stations and an emcee at rap concerts. He was a DJ for the Tottenham-based grime group Meridian Gang. During that time, he made several instrumental recordings, including “DTI (Pirate Station Anthem)” and “Private Caller.”

After Meridian Crew split up in 2005, Skepta, his brother Jme, and fellow rapper Joseph Bamber started the grime group, Roll Deep.
‘Boy Better Know,’ an English grime collective and record label, was established in 2006 by Skepta and Jme. In North London, it started as an independent record label. This record company also released the collective’s members’ music.

After the creation of “Boy Better Know,” Skepta engaged in a competition for the DVD “Lord of the Mics 2” with fellow MC Devilman. The confrontation is still recognized as one of the most important in the development of grime. After the successful match, he created a mixtape called “Joseph Junior Adenuga”.

Through the label “Boy Better Know,” he released his debut album, “Greatest Hits,” on September 17, 2007. On September 15, 2008, he published ‘Rolex Sweep’ on his website as a digital download. It was made available as the first track on his second studio album, “Microphone Champion.” ‘Rolex Sweep’ debuted at No. 86 on the UK Singles Chart.
